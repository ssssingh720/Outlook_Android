Use of Cookies

A cookie is a small file which is placed on your hard drive when you visit any of the Group's websites (directly or via our applications). We use cookies for a number of reasons.

 

  1. For statistical purposes to track how many users we have and how often they visit our websites. We collect information listing which of our pages are most frequently visited, and by which types of users.

  2. For purposes which may include identifying returning users, registrants and subscribers eliminating the need for returning users to re-enter their login details enabling you to move more easily around our applications

  3. We use other organisations to collect anonymous user information, sometimes through cookies and web beacons, (information embedded in images which allow them to analyse how the website is being used and the number of visitors).

  4. We and other advertisers may use statistical cookies to track who has seen an advertisement and clicked on it.

  5. Placing cookies on your computer or device means we can show you advertisements that you might be interested in, and it allows us to control the number of times you see them and measure how effective the ad campaign has been.

  6. We may use 'Flash' cookies to store your preferences for your media player (for example, volume and so on).

  7. We or other companies may use cookies to suggest and deliver content which we believe may interest you.

 

By using the sites you are agreeing to the use of cookies as described.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

About our advertising

 

We use online behavioural advertising (OBA). OBA allows us to deliver targeted advertising to people who visit our website. It works by showing you advertisements that are based on your browsing patterns and the way you have interacted with our site. It then shows you advertisements that may interest you.

 

As you browse our sites, some of the cookies we place on your computer and device will be advertising cookies, so we can understand what sort of pages/information you are interested in. We can then display advertising on your browser that is based on these perceived interests. For instance, if you have been surfing for mobile phones or laptops information, you may be shown such advertisements.

 

It is important to remember that none of the OBA techniques we use will collect information such as your name, email address, postal address or phone number.

 

We may also share anonymised behavioural data with advertising partners. This may mean that when you are on other websites, you will be shown advertising based on your behaviour. We may also show you advertising on our site based on your behaviour on other sites.

 

Behavioural retargeting is another form of OBA that allows us and some of our advertising partners to show you advertisements based on your browsing patterns and interactions with a site away from our sites. For example, if you have visited the website of online merchandise, you may start seeing advertisements from that same shopping site displaying special offers. This allows companies to advertise to you if you leave their website without making a purchase.

 

Advertising on mobile devices may require the collection and sharing of the unique device ID with companies who purchase advertising space on the mobile application.
