Mr Krishna Prasad:

Krishna Prasad is Editor-in-Chief of Outlook. Before joining Outlook as Editor-in-Chief he has worked for the Sunday Observer (now defunct) and the Times of India in Mumbai. He joined Outlook as a reporter and later became the magazine's Special Issues Editor. From August 2006 to June 2007, he edited the English daily Vijay Times (which was converted into the Bangalore Mirror). Since October 2008 he has been in charge of Outlook. 

Krishna Prasad along with Aniruddha Bahal exposed the match-fixing in Indian cricket .

Thomas L. Friedman described Prasad as “one of the brightest young journalists I met in India” during a 2004 visit from the United States.

Krishna Prasad has taught journalism at various colleges and universities in India and the United States for many years. From August to October 2007, he traveled in the United States, lecturing on Indian media at Central Michigan University, Ball State University, University of Wisconsin-River Falls, and Penn State University. He has also lectured at the Indian Institute of Management, Bangalore; the National Defence College, New Delhi and the Lal Bahadur Shastri National Academy of Administration.

Krishna Prasad publishes a popular blog called Churumuri, and he also operates the media blog, ‘Sans Serif”, and a food blog focused on southern Indian vegetarian cuisines.



Profile: Neelabh Mishra

Mr Neelabh Mishra is Editor of Outlook Hindi. He has over 30 years of experience in bi-lingual journalism -- Hindi and English. Apart from Outlook, Mr Mishra has been associated with or written in major publications like Navbharat Times, Times of India, Hindustan Times, Newstime, Frontline, The Week and Dharmayug.

Mr Mishra has written mainly on politics, policy, governance, development, human rights, conflict areas, and international affairs. He has been credited with many newsbreaks, investigative stories and scoops. He was also associated with the Right to Information (RTI) campaign and as such provided inputs for drafting Right to Information laws at the centre and in certain states, especially Rajasthan.

N. Mahalakshmi:

N Mahalakshmi has been with Outlook Publishing since 2007 and has over fifteen years of experience in financial research and business journalism. Prior to joining Outlook Publishing, she edited Smart Investor, the investor supplement of Business Standard, India’s second largest business daily. She is currently Editor of Outlook Business and was responsible for the re-launch in 2012 when the magazine distinctly decided to focus on Enterprise, Strategy and Markets. Under her editorship, the magazine has become the fastest growing business magazine in the country, with a circulation of 130,000 copies. Presently, Outlook Business is the second most read magazine in its genre ahead of home-bred incumbents as well as foreign titles like Forbes and Fortune.

 

Outlook Money:

Narayan Krishnamurthy is an evangelist in the media and personal finance space. For over a decade he has been analysing and writing on personal finance, stock markets, mutual funds, digital media, insurance and technology.

 

In the past, he has worked with Money Today, Fortune India and afaqs. He is a Mechanical Engineer and MBA based in Delhi.

 

Outlook Traveller:

Amit Dixit, Editor, Outlook Traveller, has worked with the magazine almost since its inception in 2001. He hit the ground running, with his first trip abroad within days of joining. He has worn many hats at Outlook Traveller and, over the years, assumed larger and larger roles. Now he looks forward to nurturing the mother brand, while being involved in dynamic brand extensions like events, curated holidays and, of course, the digital bandwagon. When he can spare a little time, he also likes to travel. In earlier avatars, he's dabbled in newspapers and book publishing.
